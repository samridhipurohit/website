About Me

I am an MPH candidate in Global Health at Emory University’s Rollins School of Public Health, with certificate training in complex humanitarian emergencies and data science. My background in dental surgery, public health, student leadership, and applied data analysis shapes how I approach health challenges: through prevention, communication, equity, and practical implementation.

My interests include diabetes, cardiovascular disease, noncommunicable disease prevention, maternal and child health, public health data visualization, and monitoring and evaluation. I am especially interested in using data and clear communication to make public health information more accessible, actionable, and useful for communities and decision-makers.

Selected Strengths

  • Clinical background in dental surgery
  • Public health data analysis using R, SAS, SQL, and GIS
  • Experience with dashboards, reporting, and data visualization
  • Monitoring and evaluation for maternal and postnatal health programs
  • Leadership, teaching support, and interprofessional collaboration

My Path

Before beginning my MPH, I trained and practiced in dentistry, where I developed a strong foundation in patient care, clinical communication, prevention, and community-based health outreach. That clinical background continues to influence how I think about public health: not only as data and policy, but also as communication, trust, access, and implementation.

At Rollins, I have expanded that foundation into global health, epidemiologic thinking, health equity, program planning, and applied public health data science. My work has included data visualization, dashboard development, public health reporting, program evaluation, and field-based learning in maternal and postnatal health.

What I Focus On

Diabetes and NCD Prevention

I am interested in how prevention, health behavior, and health systems can reduce the burden of diabetes and other noncommunicable diseases. My data work has explored how demographic, behavioral, and health-related factors shape diabetes risk patterns.

CVD and Chronic Disease Equity

I care about cardiovascular disease prevention and chronic disease disparities, especially how social conditions, access barriers, and population-level risk factors influence health outcomes across communities.

Maternal and Child Health

Through my global field experience, I worked on postnatal education, breastfeeding counseling quality, and maternal support systems. I am interested in tools that strengthen early care practices for mothers, infants, and families.

Applied Public Health Experience

My applied work has included designing a monitoring and evaluation framework for postnatal counseling, analyzing ward-level maternal and infant health data, supporting interprofessional patient recruitment through Atlanta Interprofessional Student Hotspotting, and participating in field-based public health outreach through Emory’s Student Outbreak Response Team.

These experiences have strengthened my interest in public health work that connects data, people, and systems. I enjoy turning complex information into practical outputs, whether through dashboards, evaluation tools, student initiatives, reports, or community-facing public health communication.

Leadership and Collaboration

Beyond coursework, I have served in student leadership, teaching support, and student-facing roles at Emory. These experiences have helped me build skills in coordination, project management, plain-language communication, workflow improvement, and collaborative problem-solving.

I am drawn to work that bridges technical analysis and real-world implementation, especially in settings where public health evidence needs to be communicated clearly and used for action.
